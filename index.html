Motor Vehicle Act Fines

Key penalties and spot challans under the latest Motor Vehicles Amendment Act:

Driving Without License ₹5,000

Also includes driving with an expired or suspended license.

Over-speeding (LMV) ₹1,000 - ₹2,000

Fines vary depending on the category of vehicle (larger vehicles: ₹2,000 - ₹4,000).

Drunken Driving ₹10,000 + Jail

Includes imprisonment of up to 6 months for the first offence, and ₹15,000 + 2 years for subsequent.

Jumping Red Light ₹1,000 - ₹5,000

Along with license suspension of up to 3 months.

No Seatbelt / No Helmet ₹1,000

Helmet offense also attracts a 3-month license suspension.

Mobile Phone Usage ₹1,000 - ₹5,000

Applies to handheld use while driving. Excludes hands-free navigations if securely mounted.

Your Rights (Know the Law)

Who can issue a challan?

Only a traffic police officer of the rank of **Sub-Inspector (SI)** or above can issue a spot fine (challan).

Constables and Head Constables only assist. They cannot demand fine money or write challans. If a constable stops you, ask them to call their senior officer.

Digital Documents are Valid

You are **not required** to carry physical copies of your DL, RC, Insurance, or PUC. Showing them on government-approved apps like **DigiLocker** or **mParivahan** is 100% legally valid (IT Act, Section 4).

Challan Official Receipts

Never hand over cash without receiving an **official printed receipt** from the RTO or Traffic Police department. If they demand spot cash but cannot provide a receipt, ask for a court challan instead.

Confiscation Protections

A police officer **cannot** forcefully snatch the keys out of your ignition, deflate your tires, or physically drag you out of your vehicle. Doing so constitutes an abuse of authority.

Detainment & Female Drivers

If detaining or arresting you, the police must produce you before a magistrate within 24 hours.

Crucially, female drivers cannot be detained or taken to the police station after sunset (6 PM) and before sunrise (6 AM) without a female officer present.
